# Appendices: Glossary of Terms

This glossary provides definitions for key terms and concepts used throughout the "Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics" book. Understanding these terms is essential for grasping the foundational and advanced topics discussed.

## A

-   **Actuator**: A component of a robot that is responsible for moving and controlling a mechanism or system. (e.g., motor, hydraulic cylinder).
-   **AI (Artificial Intelligence)**: The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems.
-   **ASR (Automatic Speech Recognition)**: A technology that allows computers to identify and process human speech into written text.

## B

-   **Bipedal**: Referring to an animal or robot that walks on two legs.

## C

-   **Cognitive Planning**: The process by which an intelligent agent reasons about its goals and current state to generate a sequence of actions to achieve those goals.
-   **Collision**: An event where two or more physical objects come into contact.
-   **Costmap**: A 2D or 3D grid representation of the environment used by navigation systems to store information about obstacles, inflation layers, and robot-specific costs.

## D

-   **Deep Learning**: A subfield of machine learning that uses artificial neural networks with multiple layers (deep neural networks) to learn from data.
-   **Digital Twin**: A virtual representation of a physical object or system, updated with real-time data, used for simulation, analysis, and monitoring.
-   **Domain Adaptation**: Techniques used to adapt a model trained in one domain (e.g., simulation) to perform well in another domain (e.g., real world) where data distribution might differ.
-   **Domain Randomization**: A technique used in simulation to randomize various parameters of the environment and objects to improve the transferability of learned policies to the real world.
-   **Docusaurus**: A static site generator for building documentation websites.

## E

-   **Edge AI**: Artificial intelligence processing that occurs locally on a hardware device (e.g., NVIDIA Jetson) rather than in a centralized cloud.
-   **Embodied Intelligence**: Intelligence that arises from the interaction of an agent's physical body with its environment.

## G

-   **Gait Generation**: The process of creating stable and efficient walking patterns for legged robots.
-   **Gazebo**: A powerful 3D robotics simulator.
-   **Grounding (Language)**: The process of connecting abstract linguistic concepts to concrete perceptions, actions, or objects in the physical world.

## H

-   **Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)**: The study of interactions between humans and robots.
-   **Humanoid Robot**: A robot designed to resemble the human body, typically bipedal with a torso, head, and arms.

## I

-   **IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit)**: An electronic device that measures and reports a body's specific force, angular rate, and sometimes the orientation of the body, using a combination of accelerometers, gyroscopes, and magnetometers.
-   **Inverse Kinematics (IK)**: The process of determining the joint parameters (angles or positions) that result in a desired pose and position for a robot's end-effector.
-   **Isaac ROS**: A collection of ROS 2 packages from NVIDIA that provide hardware-accelerated capabilities for robotics applications, leveraging NVIDIA GPUs.
-   **Isaac Sim**: NVIDIA's robotics simulation application built on Omniverse, providing a physically accurate and photorealistic environment.

## J

-   **Jetson Kit**: A series of embedded computing boards from NVIDIA designed for AI at the edge.
-   **Joint**: A mechanical connection between two links in a robot, allowing relative motion between them.

## L

-   **LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging)**: A remote sensing method that uses pulsed laser light to measure distances to the Earth.
-   **Link**: A rigid body in a robot's kinematic chain, connected by joints.
-   **LLM (Large Language Model)**: A type of artificial intelligence program that can generate human-like text, translate languages, write different kinds of creative content, and answer your questions in an informative way.

## M

-   **Middleware**: Software that provides services to software applications beyond those available from the operating system, facilitating communication and data management.
-   **Motion Planning**: The process of finding a sequence of valid robot movements from a start configuration to a goal configuration while avoiding obstacles and respecting robot constraints.

## N

-   **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**: A field of AI that focuses on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language.
-   **Nav2**: The ROS 2 Navigation Stack, a powerful framework for autonomous navigation in mobile robots.
-   **Node (ROS 2)**: An executable process in ROS 2 that performs computation (e.g., a sensor driver, a controller, a planning algorithm).
-   **NLU (Natural Language Understanding)**: A subfield of NLP focused on enabling computers to comprehend the meaning of human language.

## O

-   **Odometry**: The use of data from motion sensors to estimate the change in position of a robot over time.

## P

-   **Perception**: The robot's ability to sense and interpret its environment using sensors.
-   **Physical AI**: Artificial intelligence systems that interact with the physical world through a body (e.g., a robot).
-   **Physics Engine**: Software that simulates physical phenomena like gravity, collisions, and friction.

## R

-   **Reality Gap**: The discrepancy between simulation and the real world, making direct transfer of learned policies challenging.
-   **`rclpy`**: The official Python client library for ROS 2.
-   **Reinforcement Learning**: A type of machine learning where an agent learns to make decisions by performing actions in an environment and receiving rewards or penalties.
-   **ROS 2 (Robot Operating System 2)**: A flexible framework for writing robot software, providing tools, libraries, and conventions.

## S

-   **SDF (Simulation Description Format)**: An XML format for describing objects and environments for robot simulators like Gazebo.
-   **Sensor**: A device that detects or measures a physical property and records, indicates, or otherwise responds to it.
-   **Service (ROS 2)**: A request/response communication mechanism in ROS 2.
-   **Sim-to-Real Transfer**: The process of transferring a control policy or perception model trained in simulation to a real physical robot.
-   **SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)**: The computational problem of constructing or updating a map of an unknown environment while simultaneously keeping track of an agent's location within it.
-   **Synthetic Data Generation (SDG)**: The process of programmatically creating annotated data in a simulation environment for training AI models.
-   **System Identification**: The process of building mathematical models of dynamic systems from observed input-output data.

## T

-   **Topic (ROS 2)**: A named bus over which ROS 2 nodes exchange messages.
-   **Trajectory**: A path traced by a moving object.

## U

-   **Unity**: A cross-platform game engine used for real-time 3D development, including high-fidelity simulations.
-   **URDF (Unified Robot Description Format)**: An XML file format for representing a robot model in ROS.

## V

-   **VLA (Vision-Language-Action)**: The convergence of visual perception, language understanding, and robot action.
-   **VSLAM (Visual Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)**: A variant of SLAM that uses visual information (from cameras) to simultaneously build a map and localize the robot.

## W

-   **Whisper (OpenAI)**: An open-source automatic speech recognition (ASR) system developed by OpenAI.
-   **Whole-Body Control (WBC)**: A control strategy for robots that coordinates the movements of all joints to achieve a desired task while maintaining balance and respecting physical constraints.

## Z

-   **ZMP (Zero Moment Point)**: A concept used in humanoid robot locomotion to describe the point on the ground where the total moment of all forces equals zero, indicating stable balance.
